Welcome to Q2 Leagues

Q2 Leagues is a community-driven Quake II Remastered dedicated server hosted in Montreal, Canada. Whether you're a seasoned veteran or picking up the railgun for the first time, everyone is welcome to jump in and frag.

This site tracks live player statistics, Elo ratings, map history, and match results — all updated automatically as games are played on the server. Rankings are powered by a custom Elo system that rewards skill against real opponents while keeping bot matches in perspective.

The main goal here is simple: fun and enjoyment of the game. Quake II is one of the all-time greats, and Q2 Leagues exists to keep that spirit alive — fast-paced, competitive, and always a good time.

This site is still under construction and will continue to evolve with new features, stats, and improvements. Stay tuned — and in the meantime, get in there and frag!

Latest Changes to Elo

The Elo rating system is a method originally designed for ranking chess players based on head-to-head results. Each player starts at 1000 points. When you frag someone, you gain points and they lose points. The amount depends on the rating difference — beating a higher-rated player earns more points than beating a lower-rated one, and vice versa.

Applying Elo to a multiplayer free-for-all deathmatch is more challenging than a simple 1v1 scenario. In a typical map, a strong player faces multiple opponents simultaneously, which can amplify rating swings in ways that don't always reflect true skill. Balancing this is an ongoing process.

To address this, a 200-point spread cap was recently introduced for real player matchups. Here's how it works using a 1500-rated player versus a 1000-rated player as an example:

When the stronger player (1500) wins, the actual ratings are used — the gain is very small (+0.85) since the outcome was heavily expected. When the weaker player (1000) wins the upset, each player sees the opponent capped at 200 points away from themselves — so the 1000 player sees a 1200 opponent and the 1500 player sees a 1300 opponent, limiting the swing to ±12.16 instead of a much larger number.

Bot matchups use a reduced K-factor (K=2 vs K=16 for real players) so farming bots has minimal impact on your rating. Further adjustments to the Elo system may be made as the player base grows and more data is collected.

Contact the Admin

Need help or want to report an issue while in-game? Type ADMIN: your message here in the chat. This triggers an instant notification to the server administrator, who will attempt to get online as soon as possible.

If you do not wish to participate in this server's ranking system, mention this to the admin by typing something like ADMIN: please remove me from the web site in the chat. Your stats will be removed from the site. Please note that you will be expected to no longer log into the Q2 server.

Fair Play

Cheating, exploiting, and spamming are not tolerated. The server actively monitors for spam and other disruptive behavior. Hopefully this won't be too much of an issue — Q2 Leagues is built around fun and good sportsmanship. Any player found cheating or deliberately disrupting gameplay will be removed from the server and ousted from this site's rankings.
